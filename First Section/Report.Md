# Project Report: Chip Category Retail Analytics

**Prepared for:** Julia (Category Manager)
**Date:** October 2023
**Objective:** To understand customer segments and purchasing behaviour to drive the strategic plan for the chip category.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Following a comprehensive data cleaning and preparation phase (removing non-chip products, commercial wholesale outliers, and standardizing brands), we analyzed **245,255 transactions** from **71,287 unique customers**.

The analysis identifies three primary segments that drive the majority of sales. While volume is driven by older segments, a significant profit opportunity exists within the "Mainstream Young Singles/Couples" segment due to their willingness to pay a premium for specific brands.

---

## 2. Key Customer Segments by Sales

Our analysis shows that total sales are concentrated in the following lifestage segments:

1. **Older Singles/Couples:** $376,013 (Top contributor by population)
2. **Retirees:** $342,381
3. **Older Families:** $328,519

However, when we look at the **Premium Tier** intersection, the single most valuable group is **Budget Older Families** ($156,863).

### High-Level Trends

* **The Population Effect:** Older Singles/Couples and Retirees have the highest total sales because they represent the largest customer base.
* **The Loyalty Effect:** Older Families and Young Families spend the most per customer (approx. **$33.00 - $34.00**), indicating they buy chips more frequently or in larger quantities for the household.

---

## 3. Deep Dive: The "Mainstream" Opportunity

A critical insight was found in the pricing and brand preference of **Young Singles/Couples** within the **Mainstream** tier.

### Price Sensitivity

Mainstream Young Singles/Couples are willing to pay more per bag of chips than any other segment:

* **Mainstream Young Singles/Couples:** $4.07 per unit
* **Budget Young Singles/Couples:** $3.66 per unit

This **11% price premium** suggests that this segment prioritizes brand and quality over price.

### Brand Preferences

The top brands for this high-value segment are:

1. **Kettle** (3,844 transactions)
2. **Pringles** (2,315 transactions)
3. **Doritos** (2,076 transactions)

Contrastingly, **Budget Older Families** show a stronger preference for **Smiths**, a more traditional value-aligned brand.

---

## 4. Pack Size Analysis

The average pack size across all segments is consistently around **175g**.

* **Mainstream Young Singles/Couples** prefer slightly larger packs (average **178g**).
* **Older Families** show high transaction counts for **175g** but also contribute significantly to sales in larger "Family Pack" sizes.

---

## 5. Strategic Recommendations for Julia

### Recommendation 1: Targeted Premium Placement

Increase the shelf share of **Kettle and Pringles** in stores located in areas with high densities of **Young Singles/Couples**. Since this group is less price-sensitive (paying $4.07/unit), we should focus on premium flavor innovations rather than price discounts.

### Recommendation 2: Family-Oriented Value Bundles

For **Budget Older Families**, who have the highest spend per customer, implement "Multi-buy" promotions (e.g., 3 for $10) on traditional brands like **Smiths**. This rewards their high-volume purchasing behavior and maintains their loyalty to the category.

### Recommendation 3: Standardize "The Mid-Size"

Maintain the **175g** pack size as the core stock-keeping unit (SKU), as it is the "sweet spot" for all segments. However, explore expanding the 110g (Pringles) and 150-175g (Kettle) ranges for the Mainstream segments to capture higher margins.
